   patch-intrinsic behavior.

## What to pick up

The exercise generator is the weakest link and the cheapest thing to improve. `4` of `51` committed exercises
carry a `%`-format bug that raises unconditionally, and two more fail to import against their pinned image —
`6/51` wasted, and they are the only reason the raw recall reads `4/13` instead of `2/13`. A generator pass
that validates each exercise compiles and imports inside its container before committing it would remove that
whole class. **Cheap, offline, and it makes every future oracle run cleaner.** This is the natural next gate.

Beyond that, unchanged from the previous handoff and still correctly gated:

- **Benchmark release** — package the frozen benchmark + witnesses + runner as a standard eval.
- **TCP-1** — independently-authored held-out consequence tests. Now *doubly* motivated: iter231 measured the
  value-wrong class at `0/10` across two independent gold-free instruments. Needs human reviewers and budget;
  operator/resource-gated, not fakeable locally.
- **Mechanism confirmation** — the `p=0.008` fix-complexity lead, on a held-out cohort.
