# Presentation Standard

Telos should read like a record a hostile reviewer can audit.

## Rules

1. Lead with status before interpretation.
2. Separate hypotheses, results, and speculation.
3. Prefer exact nouns over promotional adjectives.
4. Put baselines, bars, falsifiers, and limitations near the claim they govern.
5. Use Mermaid diagrams only to clarify a mechanism, gate, or campaign arc.
6. Keep diagrams small enough to render on mobile.
7. Every experiment with a `RESULT.md` must be surfaced from the README.
8. Every correction stays visible in the document it corrects.
9. Nulls receive the same structure as wins.
10. The final answer of an agent is never evidence by itself.

## Voice

The voice is direct and calm:

- "No result is claimed yet."
- "This gate failed before model work."
- "The result supports this narrow claim."
- "This would falsify the next run."

Do not decorate the work. Let the receipts carry the weight.
